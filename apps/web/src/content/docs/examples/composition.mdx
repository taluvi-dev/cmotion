A composition is just a value, so a `let`-bound `compose [...]` can be placed
inside another `compose` — with its own transform, and its animations sampled
independently in each spot. That includes whole examples: each of the four
cells here is the kind of scene shown elsewhere in this gallery.

This example arranges four sub-compositions in a 2×2 grid (each `scale`d and
`translate`d into a quadrant): an icon orbit, the viking sprite walk, the
bouncing earth, and the spinning homepage glyph — 2D and 3D layers freely
mixed. A full-scene `particles` layer then sparkles over the whole grid, and
the title example — the word *cmotion*, each letter spinning in — is dropped
in the centre as the last layer, so it composites on top of everything else.
