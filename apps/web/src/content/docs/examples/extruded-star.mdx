The generic outline primitive `path(points: [vec2(x, y), ‚Ä¶])` in
`std.shapes` describes any closed 2D polygon ‚Äî here a five-point star.
`extrude(path, depth)` in `std.mesh3d` turns that outline into a solid,
`.material(...)` paints it, and `render3d(mesh, lights)` projects it to
a `Layer` that `compose` stacks over a 2D background. One directional
light, flat shaded ‚Äî no refraction or dispersion in this slice.

Paths are the non-font sibling of `text.glyph(...)`: the same
`extrude ‚Üí material ‚Üí render3d` pipeline the title sequence uses, but
the outline is points you supply rather than a glyph from a font.

This example pins `runner "0.0.3"`, the first runner generation that
renders extruded paths. Earlier runners (`0.0.1`, `0.0.2`) stay
byte-for-byte stable; raising the pin is how a source opts into the new
primitive.
