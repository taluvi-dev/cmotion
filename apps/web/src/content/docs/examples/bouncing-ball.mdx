An Earth-textured `sphere` bouncing in front of a starry-night
`image` background. The bounce, spin and squash are all driven by
looping `animate` blocks — the squash peak is timed to the floor
contact so the ball compresses on impact. Lit by an ambient +
directional pair through `render3d`.

`image(...).as_texture(...)` paints the sphere; `image(...).fit(cover)`
becomes the scene background. The squash is anisotropic — y compresses
by `factor`, x/z widen to roughly preserve volume. The eventual
`bounce(...) / on_event(...)` primitives in the sampler will let you
write this without the hand-tuned keyframe ramp.
